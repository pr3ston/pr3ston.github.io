Important People

Joseph Smith Jr.

Joseph Smith Jr. restored the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. He was born on December 23, 1805 in Sharon, Vermont. He was visited by God the Father and Jesus Christ in 1820, which was the beginning of the restoration of the Church. He was also visited by an angel named Moroni, who told him about the golden plates that contained the Book of Mormon. He translated the Book of Mormon and organized the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints on April 6, 1830. He was eventually martyred on June 27, 1844 in Carthage, Illinois.

Oliver Cowdery

Oliver Cowdery was the cousin of Joseph Smith Jr. He was the first person to be baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. He was also the scribe for Joseph Smith Jr. during the translation of the Book of Mormon. He was also one of the Three Witnesses of the Book of Mormon. He died on March 3, 1850 in Richmond, Missouri.

Emma Smith

Emma Smith was the wife of Joseph Smith Jr. She was born on July 10, 1804 in Harmony, Pennsylvania. She was the first person to be baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. She was also the first Relief Society President. She died on April 30, 1879 in Nauvoo, Illinois.

Hyrum Smith

Hyrum Smith was the older brother of Joseph Smith Jr. He was born on February 9, 1800 in Tunbridge, Vermont. He was the Assistant President of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. He was also one of the Eight Witnesses of the Book of Mormon. He was martyred with Joseph Smith Jr. on June 27, 1844 in Carthage, Illinois.
